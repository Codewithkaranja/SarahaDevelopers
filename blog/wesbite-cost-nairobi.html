Website Cost in Nairobi (2026)

Real pricing ranges, what affects cost, and what you should receive — without the fluff.

Feb 26, 2026 14 min read Nairobi / Kenya
Get a Quote Jump to Packages

In Nairobi, you’ll see website quotes ranging from “cheap and fast” to premium builds that look and perform like a real brand asset. The truth is simple: a website price is mainly determined by strategy, design quality, pages, features, content, and performance. If the quote doesn’t clearly mention deliverables, you’re paying for guessing.

Reality check: A “website” is not the product — the product is leads + trust + conversions. A good build pays for itself when it turns traffic into calls, WhatsApp and form inquiries.

2) Website price ranges in Nairobi (2026)

Below are realistic ranges most serious teams use in Nairobi. Final pricing depends on scope, content readiness, and whether you need e-commerce or custom workflows.

Starter (1–3 pages): fast business landing site + WhatsApp/calls + basic SEO setup.
Standard (5–10 pages): full business site + service pages + stronger UI + better performance.
Premium (10–20+ pages): brand-centric UI + SEO structure + custom sections + advanced tracking.
E-commerce: product catalog + payments + delivery logic + SEO + security + admin training.
Custom systems: portals, dashboards, automation, integrations, user roles, data logic.

4) Timelines (how long websites take)

Timelines depend on feedback speed and content readiness. Here’s a realistic build schedule for Nairobi teams:

Days 1–2: discovery + sitemap + content checklist + brand direction.
Days 3–7: homepage + key pages build + mobile polish.
Days 7–14: revisions + speed optimization + SEO basics + tracking setup.
14–30+ days: e-commerce/custom features, testing, integrations, launch + training.

5) What you should receive (non-negotiables)

Even a “simple” site should ship with these:

Responsive UI: looks premium on mobile + desktop (not cramped or broken).
Conversion setup: WhatsApp/calls/forms placed properly + clear CTA copy.
Speed basics: optimized images, lean scripts, decent Lighthouse performance.
SEO foundations: titles/meta, schema, indexability, internal linking, sitemap basics.
Tracking: analytics + conversion events (WhatsApp/call/form clicks).

6) What to avoid when pricing a website

  • “One price for everything” with no deliverables list.
  • Copied content (hurts trust + SEO + legal risk).
  • No speed work (Kenyan sites often get heavy and slow fast).
  • No tracking (you can’t improve what you don’t measure).
  • No support plan (sites need updates, backups, and fixes).

FAQs

How much does a website cost in Nairobi?

It depends on pages, design quality, and features. Business sites are priced based on conversion structure, speed, SEO foundations, and support — not just a template install.

Why are some website prices so low?

Usually template installs with weak mobile UX, slow speed, and little to no SEO or tracking. They often lead to rebuilds or poor lead performance.

What should be included in a website package?

Responsive design, speed optimization, on-page SEO setup, lead capture (WhatsApp/calls/forms), analytics tracking, basic security, and clear support/training after launch.
