1) Average website cost in Kenya (2026)

Here’s a realistic range most Kenyan businesses can expect:

Basic business website (1–3 pages) KES 15,000 – 35,000
Professional company website (5–10 pages) KES 35,000 – 80,000
Portfolio / personal brand website KES 25,000 – 60,000
E-commerce website (payments + products) KES 50,000 – 150,000+
Custom web apps (portals, dashboards, automation) KES 120,000 – 500,000+

2) What determines website pricing?

✅ Number of pages

More pages = more design work, content structure, and development time.

✅ Design quality (template vs premium)

A premium UI/UX build costs more than a template — but it converts better and builds trust.

✅ Features and functionality

Features increase cost because they require development + testing. Common add-ons:

  • Booking systems and appointment forms
  • Online payments (M-Pesa, Card)
  • Admin dashboards and portals
  • Live chat, CRM and email integrations
  • Automation (quotes, WhatsApp flows, reports)

✅ Content creation

If your developer writes service pages, structures content for SEO, and provides visuals, the price increases — but the end result is much stronger.

✅ SEO setup (this is where many cheap websites fail)

SEO is often the difference between “a website that exists” and “a website that brings leads”. Proper SEO includes titles/descriptions, schema, internal links, speed, indexing support and a content plan.

3) Domain + hosting costs in Kenya

  • Domain: typically KES 1,000 – 3,000 per year (varies by extension like .co.ke / .com)
  • Hosting: typically KES 4,000 – 15,000+ per year depending on speed and reliability
Rule: If hosting is too cheap, expect slow loading, downtime, and security issues — which can kill SEO and conversions.

4) Should you choose the cheapest option?

The cheapest website is often expensive later. Common issues with very cheap builds:

  • Poor mobile responsiveness
  • Slow loading pages (heavy images, no optimization)
  • No proper SEO structure (missing schema, weak headings, no internal linking)
  • Weak security practices
  • No support after delivery

5) A smart budget strategy (what to prioritize)

If your goal is growth, prioritize:

  • Professional design + strong branding
  • SEO setup + speed optimization
  • Clear service pages + conversion-focused CTA (WhatsApp/Call)
  • Google Business Profile optimization (for local leads)

7) FAQs

What is the cheapest website you can get in Kenya?

A clean one-page/landing website can start around KES 15,000–25,000 depending on design quality, hosting setup and basic SEO.

How much does an e-commerce website cost in Kenya?

Most e-commerce websites range from KES 50,000–150,000+ depending on product count, payment setup (M-Pesa/card), delivery logic and admin features.

Why do website prices vary so much?

Prices vary based on design quality, pages, features (booking, payments, portals), content creation, SEO depth, hosting level, and after-launch support.

Do I need monthly payments after the website is built?

You typically pay yearly for domain and hosting. Optional monthly costs can include maintenance, updates, SEO content, security monitoring and support.
